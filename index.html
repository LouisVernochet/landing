The history of Jaeger-LeCoultre

Since its establishment in 1833 by Antoine LeCoultre in Le Sentier, Jaeger-LeCoultre has emerged as a benchmark in luxury watchmaking, combining innovation with exceptional craftsmanship. The collaboration with Edmond Jaeger starting in 1903 gave birth to the Jaeger-LeCoultre brand, marking the beginning of a history of creativity and excellence. The Reverso, iconic with its reversible case, and the Atmos, a mechanical clock without electricity, are among the innovations that have shaped the brand's reputation.
Over the decades, Jaeger-LeCoultre has solidified its status as an horological icon, thanks to its constant commitment to innovation and quality. The brand has also established partnerships in cinema, motor racing, and art, showcasing its influence beyond watchmaking. By awarding the Glory to the Filmmaker prize at the Venice International Film Festival, Jaeger-LeCoultre celebrates art in all its forms, thus embodying its commitment to creativity and innovation. Thus, Jaeger-LeCoultre continues to make history in watchmaking with its iconic creations and legacy of excellence.
